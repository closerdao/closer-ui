# Closer Strategy

## Target problem

Land and the legal structures around it concentrate with profit-seeking capital. Each cycle, more commons get fenced, priced, and routed toward extraction, and the place-based communities that steward them lose footing. The same enclosure logic is now moving up the stack: as AI shifts from a tool to an infrastructure layer, it concentrates in the same hands that already enclosed the land. A regenerative future needs commons that hold at both layers, with communities owning the ground they live on and governing the intelligence that increasingly mediates it.

Those communities also lack working software. The defaults (Airbnb, Notion, Stripe, Discord, a multisig, a Webflow site) cannot model shared land, long horizons, non-cash contribution, transparent collective assets, or governance weighted by real investment in place. Workarounds multiply, values drift, institutional memory leaks, and coordinators burn out. The commons stays informal and fragile while the extractive stack keeps scaling.

## Our approach

Closer is a federation of land projects on a shared protocol. Communities that participate earn governance stake in that protocol, so the system steers toward its users rather than its shareholders.

The product is built around three primitives: programmable **access** (who can show up and on what terms), accounted **presence** (what time and labor were given to a place), and legible **governance** (how collective decisions and assets are recorded and enforced). These map to the on-chain instruments communities actually need, and they replace the duct-taped stack with one operating layer.

We bias product toward non-addictive, low-friction paths that deepen human connection. We refuse to operate as a marketplace in the Airbnb mold that commodifies more planetary surface and more relationships for rent.

## Who it's for

**Primary: founding stewards launching a co-invested regenerative land project.** For example, operators starting a co-living ecoretreat centered on wellness and collective infrastructure. They hire Closer to convene co-investors, keep land sovereign and protected long-term, preserve community voice as the project evolves, and run one integrated operating layer for guest journeys, presence accounting, and governance instead of duct-taping generic tools.

**Secondary: existing land projects migrating off the duct-taped stack.** Intentional communities, ecovillages, retreat centers, and land trusts whose stack has stopped scaling with their governance and economics.

**Not for: extractive operators.** Closer is not the lowest-cost path to a generic short-term rental business. Operators optimizing for rent extraction from land or relationships should use other tools.

## Key metrics

- **Federated land projects live on Closer.** Communities with sustained operational and governance participation, not one-off signups. Counted on hosted activity, on-chain governance signals where applicable, and at least one full member onboarding cycle. Source: internal project records and chain events.
- **Member activity that reflects belonging.** Monthly active members across communities, counted against core actions (stays, membership, governance votes, contribution logged), not raw logins. Source: product analytics.
- **Economic flow through communities.** Aggregate and per-community monthly value moving through the four payment rails (fiat, card, stablecoin, native tokens) across bookings, donations, token sales, event tickets, and other instruments. Source: payments stack, chain events, finance reporting.
- **Capital formation milestones.** Communities reaching defined fundraising milestones such as completed token sales or closed capital targets. Source: pipeline data, chain events, finance reporting.
- **Stewardship durability.** Land-sovereignty mechanisms holding across the portfolio (rights-of-nature clauses, anti-flip covenants, community-veto rights triggered and respected). Source: governance records and legal filings.
- **Ecological outcomes (partner-verified).** Portfolio-level signals such as carbon via Open Forest Protocol, biodiversity via eDNA, and hydrology and water-table change, sourced from partner attestations rather than claimed as first-party measurement.

## Tracks

### 1. One-click deployments

Any community can sign up on closer.earth and stand up a configured Closer instance, including a guided path to a custom site and tailored pages, without a bespoke build.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Lowers the fixed cost of joining the federation and replaces brittle one-off stacks with shared, values-aligned rails. This is what makes "federation" real instead of rhetorical.

### 2. Cross-village portability and network effects

A federation layer where a user opts into a Passport, ports relevant identity, presence history, and reputation across communities, and participates in cross-community subscriptions. Communities, in turn, gain access to a wider trusted pool of members.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Makes federation legible in user experience and economics, not only in governance diagrams. Network effects compound across villages instead of inside one tenant.

### 3. Lifecycle progression and learning loops

Members visibly progress from visitor, to recurring guest, to member, to contributor, to steward. The platform meets each step with the right next action (a stay, an agreement, a contribution log, a governance event), instruments real outcomes, and feeds the learning back into product. Touchpoints are timely and honest, not addictive.

_Why it serves the approach:_ Closes the coordination gap over time while keeping product discipline non-extractive. The unit of optimization is member depth in a place, not session length.

## Anti-goals

- We do not build an open consumer marketplace for transient stays.
- We do not run an attention-maximizing feed.
- We do not host communities that refuse legible governance or stewardship covenants.
- We do not chase scale at the cost of the protocol stake structure.

## Horizon and sequencing

- **Now (0 to 6 months):** Track 1 reaches production readiness for the next cohort of communities. Track 2 ships its first end-to-end cross-village Passport flow. Track 3 instrumentation is in place for at least three communities.
- **Next (6 to 18 months):** Federation grows to a defined count of live communities. Cross-village subscriptions and reputation become routine. Lifecycle progression patterns are documented and re-used across tenants.
- **Later (18+ months):** Federation reaches scale where stewardship outcomes and capital formation milestones compound across communities, and the protocol is meaningfully governed by its participants.

## Marketing

**One-liner:** Closer is proptech infrastructure for land projects, shifting ownership toward stewardship with programmable access, presence, and governance, and software that defaults to human connection over extraction.

**Positioning vs alternatives:** Where the default stack treats communities as users of generic tools and land as inventory, Closer treats communities as co-owners of a protocol and land as a commons. Where Airbnb-class marketplaces optimize for transactions per relationship, Closer optimizes for depth per relationship in a place.
